Obstacle Avoidance

Custom Python code processes ultrasonic sensor data to detect obstacles within 30 cm, enabling the car to stop, scan, and reroute.

×

Line Following

Infrared sensors guide the car along black lines, ensuring precise navigation on set routes.

×

Video Streaming

The ESP32-S3 camera streams real-time JPEG images, viewable remotely via WiFi.

×

Servo Control

A servo motor adjusts the ultrasonic sensor’s angle, improving obstacle detection range.

Ethics & Societal Impacts

Autonomous Vehicles

Key Ethical Considerations

Beyond Hypothetical Dilemmas

Ethical issues with self-driving cars extend far beyond rare crash dilemmas. More urgent concerns relate to safety standards, surveillance, over-reliance on automation, and societal inequality.

🔒
Public Acceptance

AVs may need to reduce fatalities by 75–80% to gain societal trust.

🚶‍♂️
Over-Reliance

Pedestrians may act recklessly, assuming the car will always stop.

⚖️
Equity and Access

Advanced safety systems could lead to traffic segregation.

🛰️
Surveillance Risks

Navigation data may be exploited for political or commercial use.

💣
Malicious Use

AVs could be hacked or used for criminal or terrorist purposes.

Societal Trade-offs

Introduction of AVs will involve trade-offs between safety, privacy, responsibility, and infrastructure control. Policymakers must balance these in ways that serve the public interest.

Ethical design choices must consider realistic road behavior, not just philosophical thought experiments. Ensuring transparency, fairness, and inclusive design will be critical to widespread adoption and societal benefit.

Source: Hansson, S. O., Belin, M.-Å., & Lundgren, B. (2021). Self-Driving Vehicles—An Ethical Overview. Philosophy & Technology. https://doi.org/10.1007/s13347-021-00464-5

×

Public Acceptance

Despite potential safety benefits, public expectations may be unreasonably high. AVs might need to dramatically outperform human drivers to be socially accepted.

×

Over-Reliance

Trusting automation too much can lead to risky pedestrian behavior, potentially reducing overall traffic safety and requiring stricter regulations or public education.

×

Equity and Access

Only high-end AVs may have the most advanced features, creating unequal access and potentially segregated roadways based on socioeconomic status.

×

Surveillance Risks

Collected data could be misused for targeted advertising, state surveillance, or manipulation, raising significant privacy and ethical concerns.

×

Malicious Use

AVs could be exploited by criminals or terrorists to carry out attacks or disrupt infrastructure, necessitating strict cybersecurity and regulation.
